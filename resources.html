Reference Guide

  • Angler Phishing – Category of a phishing attack. Attacks by spoofing customer service accounts on social media or through a company.

  • Baiting (physical) – Leaving physical media with malicious software available in a public place.

  • Baiting (online) – Enticing a victim to click a link through the promise of a reward.

  • Business Email Compromise – Category of a phishing attack. Attacks come in the form of emails masquerading as members of an organization or company.

  • Clickjacking – Misdirecting a victim into thinking they are clicking an appropriate link.

  • Diversion Theft – Altering or stealing the delivery destination of a shipment of goods.

  • Eavesdropping – An attack where a perpetrator gets information by listening.

  • Honeypot – A social engineering attack that plays off a victim’s romantic interest.

  • Malicious Insider (Man on the Inside) – An attack carried by someone on the inside of an organization or company.

  • Phishing – Sending messages with fake information, with the intent to steal the receiver’s sensitive information.

  • Piggybacking – Entering a restricted area by blending in with nearby authorized personnel.

  • Pretexting – Using a scenario to pressure a victim into performing a compromising action.

  • Scareware – Scaring the victim into thinking there is something wrong with their device.

  • Shoulder Surfing – An attack where a perpetrator gets information by looking at someone else's device screen.

  • Spear Phishing – Category of a phishing attack. Email or communication that targets an individual or organization.

  • Smishing – Category of a phishing attack. Phishing through the medium of (SMS) text messages.

  • Social Engineering Attack – Manipulating unsuspecting people using deception to convince them to reveal information or perform actions.

  • Tailgating – Entering a restricted area by closely following authorized personnel without being noticed.

  • Typosquatting / URL Hijacking – A malicious website set up by an attacker with a URL name matching closely to a legitimate website.

  • Quid Pro Quo – A perpetrator tries to get the victim to do a compromising action in the promise of a reward.

  • Vishing – Category of a phishing attack. A form of phishing via phone calls and/or voicemails.

  • Whaling – Category of a phishing attack. Targeting executives to take advantage of their executive powers for ransom.
